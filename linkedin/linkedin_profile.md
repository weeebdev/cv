Small sneakpeak from my side project: most common surnames in the Kazakhstani Regions [5280 × 2975] and in Central Asia in general [5280 × 3394] 🇰🇿 🇰🇬 🇺🇿 🇹🇯 🇹🇲

As usual, pure 
hashtag
#python and 
hashtag
#matplotlib were used, along with some packages for processing geometries and images. Chart type is a classical wordcloud. As far as I know, analyzing surname demographics was quite popular in the late 2010s but still popular among some demographers as a hobby. Data comes from Forebears. Depending on the country and sub-region in this list, sample size claimed to vary from ~50% of population, for Kyrgyzstan to ~2% of population, for Tajikistan. Yet, this is the most complete open database as far as I know.
Higher quality images hosted on imgur are available in comments.

What I found worth mentioning of methodology?
① Some surnames are not "normalized" - firstly, not gender-neutral (-a ending means female, in Russified versions) and secondly, not adapted to ethnicities (-ov, -ev ending across indigenous people spot historically Russified surnames while -uly /son of and/ -kyzy /daughter of/ are their traditional variants; some locals change and modify surnames depending on social and political trends). Would be nice if anyone advised me ideas for normalization.
② Data provider limits what and how we can access (f.e. bigger lists of surnames, their phonetically or semantically similar variants, attempts to automate crawling)
③ Dataset is quite obsolete, dates back to 2014, so it doesn't account for migration trends in recent decade. Yet, I assume only biggest national data vendors store any newer and more complete datasets.
